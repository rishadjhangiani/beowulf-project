BEOWULF

Beowulf is a literary classic Old English epic poem written between the years 975 and 1025.
The poet is unknown and is referred to as "Beowulf poet".



Beowulf Summary

Beowulf tells the heroic story of a cherished warrior by the name of Beowulf. The story begins in the Kingdom of the Danes, led by King Hrothgar. A monster named Grendel has been terrorizing his kingdom and Beowulf comes to King Hrothgar’s aid to defeat the monster. Beowulf is able to kill Grendel, however, this infuriates Grendel’s mother. The two battle it out, but in the bed, Beowulf comes out victorious and becomes King of the Geats. He leads for 50 years until a dragon threatens the kingdom. Beowulf and one of his kinsmen, Wiglaf, set out to battle the dragon, and although they win, Beowulf sustains fatal injuries and dies. A tower is built in his honor containing the treasure they won from the dragon and Beowulf’s ashes.

Common Themes

Revenge:
Revenge can be seen as the movitating factor for the main events that occur in the poem. After Grendel attacks the kingdom, Beowulf seeks revenge for King Hrothgar and kills Grendel. This causes Grendel's mother to want to seek revenge for her son's death and she attacks Heorot. In turn, this causes Beowulf to want to seek revenge once again and battles and kills Grendel's mother. These events can all be traced back to a common theme of revenge.
Leadership:
Beowulf emphasizes the importance of leadership and describes the qualities of a good leader. The poem showed qualities such as courage, selflessness, and loyalty to his people as a respected leader. A leader is a protector of his people and would do anything to keep them safe, just as Beowulf had done with the dragon threatening his land. In contrast, Hrothgar took a different approach to protext his people by not seeking unnecessary conflict with Grendel. Throughout the text, themes of what makes a good leader can be seen with Beowulf and Hrothgar.
